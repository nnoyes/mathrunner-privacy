Privacy Policy for Math Runner

Last updated: 24 May 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Math Runner ("the App", "we", "us") collects, uses, and shares information when you use our mobile game on iOS. The App is operated by Nigel Noyes.

1. Information We Collect

The App collects only the minimum information needed to operate gameplay, leaderboards, ads, in-app purchases, and basic analytics.

  • A player-chosen display name — submitted by you when you post a score to the global leaderboard. You may choose to skip leaderboard submission entirely.
  • Game scores and avatar selection — stored alongside your display name on the leaderboard.
  • A randomly generated device identifier — created locally on first launch. This identifier lets us recognize repeat scores from the same device without using your Apple ID or any personally identifying information.
  • Local game state — coins earned, avatars unlocked, settings, and high score. This is stored only on your device and is not transmitted off-device by the App itself.
  • Anonymous gameplay events — for example, when a run starts and ends, when you open the shop, when an ad is shown. These events do not include your display name or any personal identifiers.
  • Advertising and purchase information — handled by the third-party services listed below.

2. How We Use This Information

  • To display the global leaderboard.
  • To remember your purchases, unlocked content, and progress across sessions.
  • To show advertisements (which fund the free version of the App) and to process the optional "Remove Ads" purchase.
  • To understand how players use the App in aggregate so we can improve gameplay.

3. Third-Party Services

The App relies on the following third-party services. Each has its own privacy policy governing the data it collects:

  • Apple App Store / In-App Purchase — payment processing and receipt validation for the "Remove Ads" purchase. Subject to Apple's Privacy Policy.
  • Google AdMob — serves interstitial advertisements. AdMob may collect your device advertising identifier (subject to Apple's App Tracking Transparency permission), IP address, approximate location, and ad interaction data. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • RevenueCat — verifies and manages App Store receipts to track your purchase status. RevenueCat receives an anonymous identifier and your purchase receipts but not your Apple ID. See RevenueCat's Privacy Policy.
  • PostHog — receives the anonymous gameplay events described above. PostHog hosts data in the United States. See PostHog's Privacy Policy.
  • Supabase — hosts the leaderboard database and the optional avatar snapshot images. See Supabase's Privacy Policy.

4. Advertising and Tracking

The App shows interstitial ads served by Google AdMob between gameplay sessions. On iOS, AdMob will only use your device advertising identifier if you explicitly grant permission through Apple's App Tracking Transparency prompt. You may decline that prompt and still use the App; ads will still be shown but will be non-personalized. You can disable all advertisements at any time by purchasing "Remove Ads" from within the App.

5. Data Retention

Leaderboard records remain associated with your chosen display name and device identifier until you contact us to request deletion (see "Your Rights" below). Anonymous analytics events are retained per PostHog's standard retention. Local game state remains on your device until you delete the App.

6. Children's Privacy

The App is suitable for general audiences and is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, please contact us and we will delete that information.

7. Your Rights

You may, at any time:

  • Refuse to submit a display name to the leaderboard (the rest of the App will function normally).
  • Decline Apple's App Tracking Transparency prompt to limit ad personalization.
  • Uninstall the App, which deletes all locally stored data.
  • Contact us to request deletion of your leaderboard entry. We will remove the entry and its associated device identifier from our database within 30 days of a valid request.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the App after a change constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
